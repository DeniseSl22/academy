Setting Pathway Information Free: Overview

Background

In this quest, you will learn how to convert a published status pathway figure from the scientific literature to a pathway model at WikiPathways. The figure is depicted below and is from Linehan WM, Spellman PT, Ricketts CJ, et al. Comprehensive Molecular Characterization of Papillary Renal Cell Carcinoma. The New England journal of medicine. 2016;374(2):135-145.

The pathway depicts parts of the Glycolysis and Krebs cycle pathways. There are some key points about how the pathway is represented in the figure and how this relates to our pathway model:

  • The figure includes data overlaid on the pathway nodes. For the purposes of a pathway model, we will ignore node coloring.
  • The figure in some cases includes one RNA and one Protein node for the same gene product. We will use only one gene product node to represent both protein and RNA
  • To simplify, we will use straight interaction lines instead of curved.
