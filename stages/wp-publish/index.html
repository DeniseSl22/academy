WikiPathways: Publish Pathway

Background

Now things really get interesting... Are you ready to contribute to a scientific database? WikiPathways is wiki dedicated to biological pathways. The curated and approved pathway models at WikiPathways are used in research, data visualization and education. There are thousands of pathways already modeled and released, but there are many more thousands yet to be captured. This is where YOU come in!

Challenge

Publish your pathway model of Cholesterol Biosynthesis at WikiPathways. We will "tag" the pathway as tutorial pathway to keep it out of the regular quality assurance (QA) review protocol. But the steps are the same as those that you would perform to publish an original pathway model. You will use your published tutorial pathways in subsequent challenges.


  • To complete this challenge, log in to www.wikipathways.org
  • Click on 'Create' in the pathway menu in the left sidebar
  • Select 'Upload File' and browse to select the .gpml file from your last Metabolic Pathway challenge
  • Click 'Upload pathway'
  • You should see a message at the top, including a link to your new pathway!
  • IMPORTANT: the first thing you should do is tag the pathway as a "Tutorial Pathway".
    • Click the '+' icon under the Curation Tags header
    • Use the pull down menu to select "Tutorial Pathway"
    • Click the checkmark icon
  • Done! You are the first author of this newly published pathway! It has its own unique identifier , called a WPID, e.g., WP3917, and its own wepage URL.
  • NOTE: As a tutorial pathway, it will be automatically deleted in a 5-10 days, but in the meantime, you can practice making edits at WikiPathways on your very own pathway.
