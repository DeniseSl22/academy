Biology 101 - Metabolites

Background

Metabolites are the reactants and products of the chemical reactions that occur in cells. When we draw a chemical reaction as a diagram, it looks like a pathway: One Metabolite is converted into another Metabolite.

M1 -> M2

In cells, Gene Products help the reaction move forward. Most commonly, it is a protein that helps, or catalyzes, the reaction. Sometimes RNA catalyzes reactions. Either way, we say that Metabolite 1 is converted into Metabolite 2 and that a gene product catalyzed the reaction.
